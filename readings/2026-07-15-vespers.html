Symbolic Notes
A — The Trespasser
A man found dealing on ground not his own becomes the first breach: presence without permission, the body crossing a line the record insists was drawn.
B — The Impostor Pouch
A cookie dough wearing the wrong name is a small, sweet act of mistaken identity, a label speaking for a filling it does not contain.
C — The Fire Across the Line
A grass fire dispatched under one state's jurisdiction is discovered burning in another; the map insists on a boundary the flame never agreed to.

Each exhibit is a failure of containment — a person, a pouch, a fire — slipping past the boundary meant to hold it, and each failure was logged by someone whose job was simply to notice.

Working Theory — The Adjacency Clause

The Adjacency Clause holds that any two records filed within the same rolling window, regardless of distance or subject, exist in a state of provisional custody with one another — not causation, but proximity sufficient to transfer meaning. Tonight's three exhibits, filed across a single news cycle, share a single defect: a boundary presumed fixed that proved, on contact, to be permeable. The trespasser did not recognize the property line. The packaging line did not recognize the pouch it was filling. The dispatcher did not recognize, until arrival, that the fire had already crossed into another jurisdiction. The Clause does not ask why. It asks only whether the timing permits inheritance, and tonight it does.

The Cascade

The custody trace follows a single defect — misplaced boundary — as it migrates from a warrant to a wrapper to a wildfire.

1

Crawfordsville, Indiana — At 9:49 p.m., a man is taken into custody for occupying ground that was not his to occupy — the first recorded instance of a boundary failing to hold.

2

Chicago, Illinois — On a packaging line earlier that same week, a second boundary fails silently: a filling crosses into a pouch that was never meant to contain it.

3

The mislabeled pouches travel outward — Southern California, Southern Nevada, Arizona, Utah — carrying their false identity into four states before anyone notices the wrapper lied.

4

Gillette, Wyoming — A fire crew is sent out under one state's authority and finds, on arrival, that the fire itself has already relocated into a jurisdiction they do not hold.

5

By the time the blotter, the recall notice, and the fire log are filed within the same news cycle, the defect has completed its migration — from a man, to a food, to a flame — each one refusing to stay inside its assigned line.

The boundary was never the problem; the problem was believing it would hold.
