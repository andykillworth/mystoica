Symbolic Notes
A — The Runner
A man charged with taking something by force, then fleeing the scene on his own two feet, is the body refusing to be held by the sentence written about it. Flight, here, is not escape but proof of the charge.
B — The Sustained
An ALS call on an old road numbered for what it once carried is the quiet intervention that keeps a body inside its hours a little longer. It is custody of a gentler kind, life held open at the exact place named for passage.
C — The Unsealed
A bottle built without a child-resistant closure is a vessel that cannot keep its danger where it belongs. It waits on a shelf, honest about its contents but incapable of holding them back.

A body that will not be held, a body that must be held open, and a container that cannot hold at all — three failures of containment recorded on the same Sunday, each one a different grammar for the same verb: to keep.

Nine surfaces twice — once in the runner's age, once in the street he ran from — and one surfaces twice more, in the price of the poison and the number of the road.

Nine is the number that completes before it turns over into something new; here it marks the runner twice, as if the record itself were insisting he was already at his limit. The doubled one, meanwhile, is the smallest unit repeating — a road, a price — suggesting that what cannot be contained keeps returning to zero and starting again.

Working Theory — The Adjacency Clause

The Adjacency Clause holds that no incident is filed in isolation; every record sits within reach of another record filed in the same window, and reach is a form of custody. Under this reading, the runner on West Ninth Street, the medic unit idling on Old Route 100, and the unsealed bottle waiting on a shelf somewhere between Shenzhen and a customer's door are not separate files but adjacent clauses in a single sentence about what a container is asked to do on a Sunday afternoon, and what happens when it refuses.

The Cascade

The custody trace runs from the body that breaks free to the body that is kept from breaking, and ends at the object built to break its promise from the start.
