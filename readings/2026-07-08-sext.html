Symbolic Notes
A — The Receiver
The one charged with receiving is never the one who took. She is the vessel downstream, proof that stolen matter does not vanish but only migrates until it finds a still pool to settle in.
B — The False Report
A fabricated incident is a second theft laid over the first: it steals the true sequence of events and replaces it with a story built to survive scrutiny. Conspiracy is the architecture; the report is its façade.
C — The Retired Series
When an institution abandons its old numbering for a new one, it is quietly admitting that the old ledger has run out of room for what has already happened. The '99' series does not close because it is wrong — it closes because it is full.

Between them the three exhibits describe a single motion: an object taken, a story invented to hide the taking, and a numbering system retired because it can no longer hold the accumulated weight of everything that has passed through it.

Working Theory — The Adjacency Clause

Under the Adjacency Clause, no object retains a single, stable identity once it enters the custody chain of record-keeping; it is renamed at every hand it passes through. A stolen good becomes an item 'received.' A theft becomes an 'incident,' then a 'false incident,' then a 'conspiracy.' A trademark application becomes not a mark but a serial number, and even that number is disposable once its series fills. What Mystoica tracks today is not three crimes or one bureaucratic footnote — it is the single mechanism by which every system, criminal or administrative, keeps its books clean by renaming what it cannot keep.

The Cascade

Trace the custody of a single displaced object across today's three filings, and a five-step chain emerges.

1

Masonville, Kentucky — An item of value is taken from its rightful place and enters a stranger's custody — the first, unrecorded transfer.

2

Masonville, Kentucky — The item surfaces downstream in the hands of a receiver, who is charged not with the taking but with the holding — the second transfer, now visible to the record.

3

Ansonia, Connecticut — Elsewhere, a different actor senses that a record is coming for her and moves first: she files a false incident report, inventing an origin story to precede the true one.

4

Ansonia, Connecticut — The false report and the underlying larceny are charged together as conspiracy — the fabricated story and the actual theft now permanently bound in the same docket, inseparable.

5

Alexandria, Virginia — Behind both cases, the ledger itself changes shape: the '99' series that has logged every application for years is retired, and a '50' series opens to receive everything that comes next, stolen or otherwise.

The object was never lost. It only waited for the numbering system built to receive it.
