Working Theory — The Adjacency Clause

Under the Adjacency Clause, no incident is required to touch another in space or in cause — only in the week that contains it. A drone over a Utah ski town, a gas leak in a Delaware trailer court, and a barking dog on a Connecticut side street belong to the same file the moment they are dated within the same seven days; proximity in the ledger is proximity enough. What the Clause asks us to notice is not motive but sequence — the order in which the unseen, the unsmelled, and the unheard each demanded, briefly, to be written down.

The Cascade

The custody trace runs west to east, watcher to leak to bark, in under a week:
