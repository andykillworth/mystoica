Symbolic Notes
A — The Handoff
A body arrested under one jurisdiction's name is walked across an invisible line and received by another badge; the warrant is the leash, the transfer is the confession that no county polices itself.
B — The Ignition
Lightning does not start one fire; it seeds two dozen at once across dry country, proof that ignition stops being singular past a certain threshold of tension in the air.
C — The Vanishing
A vehicle taken in full dark and confirmed missing only by daylight marks the exact seam between what a place assumes it still owns and what it discovers it has already lost.

Handoff, ignition, and vanishing are the day's three grammars of transfer: custody moves hand to hand, fire moves cloud to ground, and possession moves owner to absence — the same current, running through three separately insulated wires.

Working Theory — The Adjacency Clause

Under the standing Adjacency Clause, any two custodial events logged within the same reporting window are presumed to share a chain of possession, whether or not the paperwork admits it; a warrant executed in Bergen County, a thunderstorm distributing fire two thousand miles west, and a Jeep gone missing before sunrise are not separate files but sequential entries in one uninterrupted ledger of things being taken and handed on.
