Symbolic Notes
A — The Ledger Theft
A grocery aisle is a small and honest economy; when a thing vanishes from it, the whole system briefly forgets what it owns. The Piggly Wiggly theft is a puncture in that trust, made at the hour when shelves are still being restocked.
B — The Flowering Blaze
A road named for flowers takes fire before six in the morning — the gentlest possible name given to the site of the day's harshest event. Twenty-nine bodies arrive to argue with what the flame insists it now owns.
C — The Displaced Filling
Chicken sealed inside a label that swears it is pasta is not a mistake of taste but of identity — a substance made to travel under a name that was never its own, discovered only when someone finally opened the tub.

Theft, fire, and false labeling are three faces of the same crime: something is not where its record says it should be, and each file is really a custody dispute in disguise.

Working Theory — The Adjacency Clause

Under the standing Adjacency Clause, any two records entered within the same rotation of the sun are held in provisional custody of one another until a causal thread can be proven entirely absent — and no such proof has yet been filed. A theft logged at a grocery counter, a fire logged on a road named for flowers, and a recall logged against a tub of falsely labeled salad share nothing on paper but the morning that contains them, which under this clause is sufficient grounds to treat them as a single unresolved file.
