Working Theory — The Adjacency Clause

Under what we have come to call the Adjacency Clause, no dispatch is ever truly isolated from the ones filed beside it in the same rotation; proximity in the record is its own form of custody, and an exhibit logged within hours of another inherits a portion of its charge whether or not the two ever occupy the same coordinates. A domestic charge tripled at a Rhode Island address, a soup shipment that skipped its checkpoint, and a sky that struck the ground 2,088 times before anyone thought to count it — these are not separate events under the Clause. They are three faces of a single uninspected threshold, recorded in three jurisdictions because no single jurisdiction could hold the whole of it.
