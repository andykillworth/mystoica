Working Theory — The Adjacency Clause

The Adjacency Clause holds that no two records filed on the same calendar day are truly unrelated — that bureaucracy, like sediment, deposits its strata in matching bands whether or not anyone is looking for the pattern. Here the clause tightens: a heater ignites when removed from water, an accomplice ignites when removed from silence, and a gas main ignites — twice — when removed from pressure. The Commission's first invocation of Section 12 in thirty-nine years is not incidental to Stonehouse's arrest three states west; it is the same clause of the same law, written in different alphabets, describing the moment a contained thing decides it no longer wishes to be contained.
