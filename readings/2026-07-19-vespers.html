Working Theory — The Adjacency Clause

The Adjacency Clause holds that no filing is ever truly isolated — that any two records entered within the same rotation of the clock share a latent circuit, and that a third record, arriving from an unrelated agency, completes rather than dilutes the pattern. Tonight's exhibits obey the clause precisely: a reversal on a federal highway, an unlicensed ignition on a residential street, and a concealed overheating inside a consumer product form a single failure of containment, expressed three times in three vocabularies — vehicular, municipal, domestic.
