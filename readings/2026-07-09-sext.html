Working Theory — The Adjacency Clause

Under the Adjacency Clause, incidents logged within the same seventy-two-hour reporting window — regardless of jurisdiction, agency, or subject matter — are treated as fragments of one suppressed event, each filing office holding a shard without knowing the shape of the whole. The clause does not require intent or contact between the parties; it requires only that the paperwork arrive close enough in time to rhyme.
