Working Theory — The Adjacency Clause

The Adjacency Clause holds that any two containment failures logged within the same reporting window become evidentiary neighbors, bound not by cause but by the mere fact of co-occurrence inside the custody trail. Under this reading, a shattered car window, a mis-sleeved power cord, and an unattended revolver are not separate incidents but a single distributed event, the record simply lacking a form field wide enough to say so.
